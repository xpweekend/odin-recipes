Spaghetti alla Carbonara

Home Old Version

Description

Carbonara is a Roman dish made with eggs, hard cheese, and cured pork. Its signature rich and silky sauce comes from beaten eggs tossed with hot pasta. The trick to making carbonara is making sure the pasta is hot enough to cook the eggs, but not so hot that they curdle.

Ingredients

  • Olive oil.
  • Guanciale (or unsmoked bacon/pancetta).
  • Spaghetti (or bucatini).
  • Eggs (3 large).
  • Pecorino Romano (or Parmesan).
  • Salt & pepper.

Steps

  1. Crisp the pork in olive oil, drain.
  2. Boil spaghetti in salted water; drain and return to pot (let cool a bit).
  3. Whisk eggs with half the cheese and pepper.
  4. Toss hot pasta with egg mixture until creamy.
  5. Stir in pork; top with remaining cheese and pepper.
